Japan 2025

9/30 - 10/14

Our plan this trip was to explore Hokkaido. We will fly into Tokyo and stay a few nights, then fly into Hakodate, rent a car and drive north to Niseko (day trip to Lake Toya), then drive to Sapporo along the coast (stop by Cape Kamui and Otaru), and finally fly back to Tokyo for a few days before flying home.

Trip Areas In Order

10/1 - 10/2

Tokyo and Kichijoji

After landing, we were immediately overstimulated with the sheer amount of shit happening in Tokyo. I have been looking forward to this trip for such a long time, and was immediately having doubts like “oh no, is this how my vacation is going to be the whole time”.

10/3 - 10/4

Hakodate

Early morning, we flew into the dreamy city of Hakodate. A coastal city that was very chill and quiet, filled with charm and beautiful views. Here we noticed that we saw a lot more elderly people than we did in Tokyo, and everyone seemed more relaxed and laid back, and most importantly, ZERO depressing salarymen. A city where we first regretted booking two nights at, became one of our favorite stops on the trip.

We woke up early and took the first train to the airport for our 7am flight to Hakodate.

Waiting for the first train

Flying AirDo, we were lucky to have a Rokon Jet which was Pokemon themed! Did some research and apparently it's a collaboration that was cancelled in 2016, but then revived in 2021. So our plane was decorated in Vulpixes (and some ice looking Vulpixes that I've never seen), and even the flight attendants wore cute Pokemon themed aprons.

It was such a beautiful morning too

We then walked toward the bay, where we had views of Mt. Hakodate as well as the sea. The weather was wonderful. Imagine SF but without 100mph winds that penetrate your bones. The water was so calm, the sky was so clear, and the best part: no crowds. Just me and Bella. A quality of Hokkaido which really made our trip special, and also something I will continue to mention in different parts of this page. Hokkaido really was a feast for the senses.

Tired, we went back to the hotel to rest up (and get an extra battery), and then walked to Fort Goryokaku Tower where we planned to watch the sunset.

The only photo of me you'll probably see on this trip

The walk from our hotel to Fort Goryokaku Tower

The tower had amazing views of the city, the bay, and the mountains. Hakodate is surrounded by these layers of green hills and mountains, and the other side is the sea. Since there weren't really any tall buildings, you can see how far the city extends and we're not even that high up. It was difficult to capture how immersive the scene was, just a 360 view of a peaceful city surrounded by nature.

Also, below there is the actual fort. It is like a star shaped garden now, that was important during the Hakodate War between some remaining shogunate forces and the new Meiji government in 1869 (We learned this from the tower's museum). We will visit here tomorrow

We ended the night with some Shio ramen (salt), a Hakodate specialty. I like lighter tasting ramens, so I really enjoyed it
