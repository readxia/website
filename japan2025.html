Japan 2025

9/30 - 10/14

Our plan this trip was to explore Hokkaido. We will fly into Tokyo and stay a few nights, then fly into Hakodate, rent a car and drive north to Niseko (day trip to Lake Toya), then drive to Sapporo along the coast (stop by Cape Kamui and Otaru), and finally fly back to Tokyo for a few days before flying home.

Trip Areas In Order

10/1 - 10/2

Tokyo and Kichijoji

After landing, we were immediately overstimulated with the sheer amount of shit happening in Tokyo. I have been looking forward to this trip for such a long time, and was immediately having doubts like “oh no, is this how my vacation is going to be the whole time”.

The plan was to go to Harajuku and buy some shoes for the trip, but after stumbling through some tourist packed areas and people watching luxury shopping, we were both kinda just disgusted with the consumeristic nature of humans. Hungry and tired, we just left without getting anything.

Fuck this street, I never want to come here again

Nonetheless, a nice wet day

Bella found us a curry place. I was so nervous to go in, but they were really friendly when we entered, and the ordering machine was really easy to use.

Joto Curry

The next day we went to Kichijoji, a city west of Tokyo, and finally we were relieved because this was the Japan we were looking forward to: quiet parks, local restaurants, cute neighborhoods, fun shopping, and just a sense of community.

Kichijoji station

Buses lining up in the morning

Got some delicious onigiri from this shop. Even though konbini onigiri is good, it's so much better from a dedicated shop

Then we went to Inokashira Park and ate our onigiri there. (fun fact: this is where I took my first date in Persona 5)

Little does she know that I've already taken 4 other girls here to this park

Found some benches and ate our out of focus onigiri

There's an urban legend where couples who take a swan boat on the lake are bound to break up

Not sure what that building is but I like the colors of the trees here

As we walked towards one end of the park and started encroaching onto the residential neighborhoods, we stopped by a konbini and got a pear sando.

We hiked across some neighborhoods to get to the other end of the park. On the other side of the park, there were many sports activities and fields, a bustling community. There was also a Studio Ghibli museum, but we didn't go since we didn't book tickets in advance.
